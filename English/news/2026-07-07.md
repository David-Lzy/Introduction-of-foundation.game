# 2026-07-07 Official hot update: Galaxy Cup Carnival, Hugo Crast, and Shadowfront QoL

## Newly confirmed today

- The official **2026-07-07** hot-update post adds a new short-term event signal:
  - **Galaxy Cup Carnival** is about to begin.
  - Players will collect **Golden Ball Coins** and redeem event rewards.
- The same update also confirms a new roster gate:
  - The new **Heroic Crew Member** **Hugo Crast** will appear only on servers that have been open for at least **27 weeks**.
- Several guild-fight and timing changes are now public:
  - In **Shadowfront**, players can vote for up to **two** available battle time slots instead of only one.
  - Main and substitute members will now receive a reminder before the **Battle Arrangement** stage ends.
  - **Ascendancy Shrines** now respawn every **20 seconds**.
  - During rallies and garrisoning, **Flagships** that are not in combat and are not affected by **War Frenzy** can now repair combat craft.
- The post also says the **Operation Blackout** event should now appear correctly in the **Event Calendar**.

## What players / officers should do

- If your server is already past the **27-week** mark, start checking whether your guild wants to plan resources or roster space around **Hugo Crast**.
- For **Shadowfront**, stop forcing members to choose only one slot in your internal prep. The official rule now allows up to **two** votes, so roster planning can be more flexible.
- Tell both main and substitute **Shadowfront** members to watch for the new **Battle Arrangement** reminder instead of relying only on manual pings.
- In **Ascendancy** races, assume **Shrine** timing is tighter now. A **20-second** respawn interval can reward faster rotations and quicker contest calls.
- If you run rallies or defensive holds, recheck your repair habits. Combat craft can now be repaired in those states only when the **Flagship** is out of combat and not under **War Frenzy**.
- If **Operation Blackout** was missing from your in-game calendar before, verify it again after the hot update instead of assuming the event is gone.
