# Flagship Development

This chapter covers flagship (hull) and component development: role positioning, cost-efficient upgrades, component choices, and upgrade logic.

---

## 1) Roles of the three flagships

Common ships:

- Gramore (starter)
- Danmosel (usually from newbie rewards/sign-in)
- Opportunity

### Opportunity: trade-specialized

Has trade-oriented passive effects (about +30% fleet movement speed during commerce), ideal for daily trade routes.

### Danmosel: combat-oriented

Generally better for fighting.

### Practical split

- 2 ships for trade (including Opportunity)
- 1 ship for combat or guild rally support

---

## 2) Flagship level cap

Flagship level is capped by energy core level.

Example: Core Lv24 usually means flagship max Lv24.

---

## 3) Galaxy coin bottleneck

Mid/late flagship upgrades are split into sub-slots and consume huge galaxy coins (often millions per slot).

Conclusion: galaxy coins are a long-term hard bottleneck.

---

## 4) Event tempo (Lv20+)

For Lv20+ players, many events score specific upgrade actions by day.

Best practice:

> Save flagship/component upgrades for the matching event day.

---

## 5) Component priorities

Path: Flagship → select ship → Components.

Typical 5 components: radar / central core / railgun / armor / thruster.

Priority:

> Central energy core first, then others.

For beginners: green set is enough as a bridge; Lv4–5 is usable.

### Build archetypes

Central core influences your combat archetype (usually 3 schools).
Heroes also follow corresponding schools.

For non-heavy spenders:

- Try using different archetypes across 3 flagships
- Spread hero investment across 3 schools to avoid one-school lock

---

## 6) Component materials

Mainly from high-level Supreme Church targets and action-point PvE.

Rule:

> Farm the highest level you can handle for better drops and shard quality.

---

## 7) Advancement and rarity

Advancement shards are expensive and slow.
Do not force it; upgrade naturally when fragments come.

Purple components are not trash:

- Can be strong mid-game bridges
- Breakthrough at higher levels can be a major jump
- May outperform low-level gold pieces temporarily

---

## 8) Fighters: unlock order and quick-use routine

### Fighters are flagship equipment, not a side system

Fighters are effectively **equipment mounted on a Flagship**.
They directly raise flagship stats and fleet rating, so they should not be postponed forever.

The practical rule is:

> **Try to match fighter style with your Flagship and hero setup.**

That keeps one combat school stacked instead of scattering value.

### Shipyard unlock order matters

The source page gives a very usable unlock line:

- **Kinetic shipyard**: fighter blueprint from **Counterattack Operation 3-6** and **energy core Lv.6**
- **Beam shipyard**: **energy core Lv.11** and **Kinetic shipyard Lv.1**
- **Ion shipyard**: **energy core Lv.13** and **Beam shipyard Lv.1**

Practical reading:

- open the first line early so you can fill fighter slots at all
- expand into Beam / Ion based on your main combat school
- do not try to level every shipyard at once without a plan

### Daily production logic

Path:

Flagship → Shipyard → **Fighter Production**

Recommended order:

1. Decide which school your main flagship is using
2. Build fighters that support that school first
3. Upgrade the shipyard before chasing stronger tiers

The page is explicit that higher shipyard levels unlock stronger fighter options.

### Equip fast first, optimize later

From the fleet screen, you can mount fighters into empty slots.
If you do not want to micro-manage yet, the safe default is to use **one-click equip** first.

For most players, the order should be:

- fill empty slots first
- then check style alignment
- only then chase higher rarity or better optimization

### More slots come from flagship level

Another useful source detail:

- **Higher flagship level increases the number of fighter slots**

So if you already own fighters but cannot mount more, check:

1. flagship level
2. whether the flagship actually has free slots
3. whether this is the ship you really plan to keep developing

---

## 9) Upgrade pattern

Because upgrade cost grows faster than stat gain, long-term value is usually from **balanced upgrades** across all 5 components (with slight early lead on central core).

---

## Summary

- Split 3 ships by role
- Respect energy-core cap
- Plan around galaxy-coin bottleneck
- Save key upgrades for event scoring days
- Central core sets archetype
- Open fighters early and match them to your main combat school
- Stronger fighters come from stronger shipyards, not just from waiting
- Farm high-level targets
- Don’t force advancement
- Use balanced component growth mid/late
