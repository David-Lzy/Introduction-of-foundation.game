# Hero Development

Heroes are a major part of combat power. Wrong investment direction wastes food and shards heavily.

This chapter covers:

- Acquisition
- Level/star growth
- Skill linkage
- Resource allocation
- Light-spend suggestions

---

## 1) How to get heroes

- Mainline gifts provide some heroes early
- Recruitment building level shortens free recruitment interval

Do not over-invest in recruitment building only for interval reduction.

### Recruitment checkpoints that actually matter

If you are still in the phase of asking where hero supply really comes from, keep these in mind first:

- standard recruitment unlocks after **Chapter 4**
- each standard pull costs **1 Voyage Memory**
- **Voyage Memory** commonly comes from Counterattack, Home Port dividends, Discount Store, Info Store, events, or a direct **1,200 Credits** purchase
- limited recruitment is separate and usually **does not** spend Voyage Memory

Practical takeaway:

> early recruitment volume is not an isolated gacha problem; it is tied to mainline pace, Home Port dividends, store choices, and event timing.

### Shard rule many players misread

The source page adds one very useful restriction:

- generic hero shards do **not** let you unlock a hero you do not already own

That makes the spending rule clearer:

- use **generic Epic shards** mostly on purple bridge heroes you are actively using
- save **generic Legendary shards** for gold heroes already confirmed as long-term core units

Stable shard sources include:

- Ruins Excavation
- Commerce Guild Store
- Glory Store

So do not over-hoard shards just because they are flexible.
If your core lineup is already clear, converting shards into real star progress is usually better.

### Best non-gacha sources to remember

If you do not want your whole hero plan to depend on pulls, these are the easiest anchor points:

- **Juri Amane**: guaranteed from day-2 of the 8-day login flow, then further supplemented through **Radiant** and events
- **Lily**: shard access through **Glory Store** and some guild-side activity rewards
- **Cocoon**: not only from recruitment, but also from **Supreme Vanguard** and some events
- **Zora**: more tied to light-spend / paid starter sources rather than routine free farming

Conclusion:

> do not spread early hero investment evenly; lock in the heroes you can support through login rewards, events, and stable store exchange first, then decide whether limited banners are worth extra spend.

---

## 2) Priority: gold heroes first

Rule:

> Prioritize gold 5-star heroes.

Purple heroes are for transition; avoid heavy long-term spending on them unless no good gold options exist.

---

## 3) Level cap linkage

Hero max level is tied to energy core level.
Late game often has food surplus but level cap bottleneck.

---

## 4) Recommended roster size

Each flagship uses 3 heroes.
3 ships = 9 heroes.

Recommended:

> Invest in 9 core heroes only.

---

## 5) Purple hero note

Clara (system-given) is often efficient due to easier shard access.
Some other purple heroes can stay as short-term bridge only.

---

## 6) Stars and skills

Growth has two axes:

1. Levels (food)
2. Stars (shards)

Skill level caps are tied to star level, so star upgrades matter.

---

## 7) Rare shard heroes

For heroes with very rare shards, avoid pre-hoarding all resources.
Wait until acquisition is real, then decide investment.

---

## 8) Hero pass (light spending)

Path: top-right event icon.
Complete dailies for activity points; paid track grants extra shards/rewards.

Usually high value for newer players with low star heroes.

---

## 9) Food usage tempo

- Early game: invest steadily
- Mid/late: keep reserves

Reason: some events (e.g., VS) score upgrade consumption on specific days.

---

## 10) Luminous-orb relation

Luminous Orb is an important food source. Do it daily when possible.

---

## Summary

1. Gold first
2. Raise 9 core heroes
3. Purple = transition
4. Star level gates skills
5. Use food by phase
6. Hero pass is often good value for beginners
