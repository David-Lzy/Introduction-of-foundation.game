# Guild PvP Rules and Safety

This page organizes a guild announcement into practical PvP rules for daily play.
It is especially useful for new members before entering contested space.

---

## 1) Hard rules: do not attack these targets

Do **not** attack the following under normal circumstances:

- **MIT guild ships** — they are marked as allies
- **Cargo ships**
- **Ships participating in rallies or event tasks**
- **Any target located in non-PvP zones**

If you are unsure whether a ship is part of an event or rally, treat it as protected and do not hit first.

---

## 2) Safe and unsafe star levels

According to the guild notice:

- **Star levels 4, 6, 7, and 8** are considered **PvP areas / unsafe zones**
- Non-PvP zones should be treated as protected areas

Practical takeaway:

- Enter star 4/6/7/8 with the expectation that combat can happen
- Do not park vulnerable fleets there if you cannot monitor them
- Use safer zones when you are farming casually or stepping away soon

---

## 3) Offline safety habit

Before going offline:

- **Return fleets to base**

Why this matters:

- Fleets left outside in unsafe areas can be attacked while you are away
- A simple recall before logout reduces avoidable losses

This is one of the highest-value low-effort PvP habits for new players.

---

## 4) Guild communication expectations

The guild expects members to:

- Read announcements regularly
- Monitor guild communications
- Respond when leadership sends a direct message
- Join events when available

If a player never responds and never participates, promotion can stall.
In the original notice, inactive members were said to remain below **R1 promotion threshold**.

---

## 5) Recommended beginner PvP checklist

Before you attack anyone, quickly confirm:

1. Is the target an ally or allied guild ship?
2. Is the target a cargo ship?
3. Is the target busy with a rally or event?
4. Is the fight happening in an allowed PvP zone?
5. Are you about to log off soon?

If any answer creates doubt, stop and re-check first.

---

## 6) One-paragraph summary

Good PvP discipline is not only about winning fights. It is also about avoiding diplomatic mistakes, respecting protected targets, staying alert in unsafe star systems, and recalling fleets before going offline.
