# Glory Arena

This page focuses on the rules that matter most in Glory Arena.

If you treat it as a throwaway "tap three times and leave" routine,
you usually miss a lot of **Glory Coin** value and rank progression.

---

## 1. What kind of mode this is

Glory Arena is:

> **3 flagships vs 3 flagships** in auto PvP.

The key rules are:

- you recover **3 tickets per day**
- both wins and losses still give some **Glory Coin**
- wins raise your points and rank
- losses **do not reduce points**, but still spend a ticket
- a full season lasts roughly **2 weeks**

So this is not a "only fight when you are sure" mode.
It is a long-cycle mode where you generally want to spend your daily tickets.

---

## 2. Why it is worth clearing daily

Because the mode gives three layers of value:

1. **Glory Coin**
2. rank-up rewards
3. season ranking rewards

The source page is explicit that even a loss is not empty.

For most players, the real mistake is not losing.
It is **leaving tickets unused**.

---

## 3. Minimum prep before entering

### 1) You need three usable fleets, not one

Glory Arena uses **3 flagships** in one run.

That means you cannot rely on only one fully built lineup.
You need to think in terms of:

- three fieldable flagships
- at least **9 usable champions**

If all your strength is concentrated into one ship and three champions,
this mode punishes that quickly.

### 2) Prioritize space-combat skills

The source page specifically notes that Glory Arena is **space combat only**.

So when you choose where to spend upgrades,
space-combat skill gains should move up your priority list.

---

## 4. How to choose opponents well

Each challenge gives you 3 opponents to pick from.

The reliable method is to look at two things together:

1. **is their power below yours?**
2. **does the win reward enough points to be worth it?**

The best order is:

> **remove the obvious bad fights first, then choose the highest-point target among the fights you can probably win.**

That is more stable than chasing only points or only the weakest target.

---

## 5. Use your free refreshes first

For each challenge, you get **3 free opponent refreshes**.

That means:

- do not force a bad fight if the current list is poor
- spend the free refreshes before thinking about anything else

After that, the source page says you can keep refreshing by spending **Credits**.

The better default order is:

1. use the free refreshes first
2. spend **Credits** only when you are close to a rank threshold or actively pushing leaderboard placement

---

## 6. Team-building principle: make all three fleets real

The source page includes example lineups by energy type,
but the most important practical rule is simpler:

> **each flagship should have a real same-system lineup behind it—do not stack everything into fleet one.**

This is a 3-fleet mode, not a single-arena duel.

If your first fleet is excellent and the other two are hollow,
your win rate will stay unstable.

---

## 7. Best routine for most players

If you are not a heavy spender pushing the top ranks, the efficient routine is:

1. spend your **3 daily tickets**
2. prioritize fights you can win consistently
3. keep converting Glory Coin into long-term value
4. grow all three flagships and all 9 core champions together

In practice:

- early season matchmaking is usually easier
- later, when ranks rise, you can decide whether extra investment is still worth it

---

## 8. Fast execution checklist

Before you queue, check this:

1. all three flagships are actually equipped
2. no obvious empty or weak champion slots across the 9 slots
3. space-combat skill priorities were reviewed
4. target lower-power opponents with good point returns
5. use all 3 free refreshes if the board looks bad
6. after the run, check your Glory Coin and rank progress

---

## One-line takeaway

**Glory Arena rewards players who build three playable fleets and spend tickets consistently, not players who only spike one lineup.**
