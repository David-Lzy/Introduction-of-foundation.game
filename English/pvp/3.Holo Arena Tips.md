# Holo Arena Tips

This page turns SuzieQ's arena notes into a cleaner reference you can scan before spending attempts.

---

## Choosing opponents

Check the player's full profile before you fight. Fleet rating alone can be misleading.

### Targets to avoid first

- **Players showing only one ship**
  - They may come back later with a full fleet.
  - That often means you lose points again and also give them revenge points.
- **Players with a high-level flagship but weak support ships**
  - This can hide a much stronger real setup than their surface rating suggests.

### What to compare besides fleet rating

Look at:

- Ship levels
- Component levels
- Champion levels
- Number of legendary champions

Treat fleet rating as only one signal, not the final answer.

### Refresh carefully

Use refresh only when you truly do not have a viable target.
A bad reroll can leave you with even worse options.

---

## Ship formation

Formation matters a lot in Holo Arena.

### Before entering

- Check the matchup strengths of the ships involved.
- If you forgot, back out and re-enter before committing.

### Front ship priority

Try to make sure your **front ship can beat their front ship**.
If you win that first trade, your surviving front ship helps you pressure the other two enemy ships and gives you a strong tempo advantage.

At the same time:

- Do **not** waste your strongest ship on the front if a weaker one can do the same job.

### Hidden side stacking

Some players stack ships behind one side ship, making the real layout hard to read.
If you think that positioning trick was the main reason you lost, adjust your formation and use a rematch attempt.

---

## Rematch

### What rematch does

- Rematch is the **top-left document icon**.
- You can replay the same opponent **once per day**.
- Winning can recover lost points and also grant revenge points.

### Re-check before fighting again

If you upgraded since the first fight, the opponent may have upgraded too.
Click their **avatar** again and re-check the actual setup instead of assuming the old result still applies.

### Useful side effect

According to the original note, playing a rematch resets your **free refreshes back to 3**.
That means sacrificing a small number of points can sometimes be worth it if it helps you roll into better main-challenge opponents later.

---

## Extra practical tips

- Early in the event, when many opponents are beatable, buying extra attempts after the free ones can be reasonable.
- Later on, paid attempts are much less efficient if the lobby is full of opponents you cannot beat.

---

## One-line takeaway

**Do not trust fleet rating alone—check profiles, plan formation, and use rematch strategically.**
