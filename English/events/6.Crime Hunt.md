# Crime Hunt

This page focuses on one thing:

> **Crime Hunt is less about manual combat and more about keeping your `Agent Signal` flow, clue conversion, and rally timing clean.**

If the order is right, the event becomes a steady source of Juri Amane fragments and training materials.

---

## 1. What Crime Hunt actually asks you to do

The event loop is simple:

1. farm `Agent Signal`
2. spend it in the event panel to roll clues
3. use tracking to locate the target
4. rally and kill the target

When players stall here, it is usually not a power issue.
It is usually because the signal → clue step was too slow.

---

## 2. Where to get `Agent Signal` first

The source page points to two main paths:

- **Merchant Requests**: the preferred main source
- **Supreme Vanguard**: useful for extra volume, but usually less efficient

For most players, the cleanest routine is:

> **clear the Merchant Requests you already wanted to do, then decide whether to spend extra Action Points on Supreme Vanguard.**

That keeps your AP flow stable.

---

## 3. Do not leave signals unused

`Agent Signal` does not convert automatically.

You need to open the event page and use it manually.
That is what gives you a chance to pull clues.

Even when you miss a clue, you can still get side value such as:

- training manuals
- speedups
- other growth materials

So a bad roll is not a total dead run.

---

## 4. How to fight once the target is tracked

After you get a clue, hit **Track** to locate the target in the same star system.

Three practical points matter here:

### 1) Treat it as a rally-first target

The target supports rallies,
and it **does not disappear immediately if the first attempt fails**.

That means you can:

- add more members
- relaunch
- finish it in a cleaner second push

### 2) Send your strongest flagship

The source page states that, like Tribute Ships,
this target can be challenged with **only one flagship at a time**.

So do not throw in a trade ship or utility ship by habit.
Send the ship with your best real combat development.

### 3) Check total rally HP before launching

The most useful hard number from the source is:

- aim for **450,000+ total rally HP**

Below that, the fight is not automatically doomed,
but your failure rate rises fast.

---

## 5. Combat format: space battle, fully auto-capable

Crime Hunt uses **space combat**, not ground combat.

It also supports:

- **auto battle**

So the real prep checklist is not manual play.
It is:

1. is the ship strong enough?
2. is the rally big enough?
3. did you send the right flagship?

---

## 6. Reward limits players miss most often

### Rally starter reward

If you start the rally and the target dies, you get the starter reward.

### Special starter reward

For the first **5 successful rally starts per day**,
you also get an extra reward layer.

The biggest ones to remember are:

- **Juri Amane fragments**
- **Legendary Training Manuals**

### Participation reward

Joining successful rallies started by other players gives participation rewards,
up to **50 times per day**.

The practical takeaway:

> **if you stay online for a while, do not only start your own rallies—join guild launches too.**

---

## 7. Rewards go to mail, not straight into your bag

This is easy to misread as a bug.

Crime Hunt rewards arrive through **mail**,
not as instant on-kill loot.

Check mail after a session so you do not leave fragments and manuals unclaimed.

---

## 8. Best simple execution order

If you just want a clean daily sequence, do this:

1. clear **Merchant Requests** first for `Agent Signal`
2. spend signals as soon as you have enough
3. track the target immediately after getting clues
4. send only your strongest flagship
5. check whether rally HP is close to or above **450,000**
6. collect rewards from mail
7. use spare online time to fill participation rewards

---

## One-line takeaway

**Crime Hunt is mostly a signal-priority, single-flagship, and rally-HP management event.**
