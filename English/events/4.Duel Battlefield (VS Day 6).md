# Duel Battlefield (VS Day 6)

> Audience: MIT2 / HuaXia officers and members  
> Role: high-intensity PvP final push during VS Day 6  
> Updated: 2026-05-02

## One-line summary

**Duel Battlefield is not a free-for-all. It is the highest-risk scoring phase of VS Day 6: fighters must follow command, and non-fighters must shield so they do not feed points.**

---

## 1. Event role

Duel Battlefield is the Day 6 combat layer of VS / Guild Duel. Its usual traits are:

- high PvP pressure
- key objective structures such as War Core and War Fortress
- direct attacks on enemy players in some windows
- last-stage score racing

It is therefore very different from earlier VS days focused on construction, research, spending, or development release.

---

## 2. The three officer decisions that matter first

### 2.1 Time windows

The screenshots show short and long battle windows. Guild notices should always make clear:

- which time zone is being used
- when War Core opens
- when War Fortress opens
- which window is the main attack window

### 2.2 Who fights and who does not

The biggest risk is often not low damage. It is poor separation between fighters and non-fighters:

- low-power members exposed for free enemy points
- non-fighters forgetting to shield
- main fighters scattered instead of grouped

That means officers should split members into two categories:

- **fighters**: online early, grouped, under command
- **non-fighters**: shielded, fleets recalled, not exposed

### 2.3 Diplomacy / no-hit rules

If there are diplomacy constraints, define them before combat starts:

- which targets are valid
- which targets are off-limits
- who can call stop-fire or retreat

---

## 3. Expectations for members

### Fighters

Fighters should:

- be online 10 minutes early
- prepare main fleets, repairs, teleports, and speedups
- stay on guild chat / voice
- avoid solo chasing
- avoid starting unsanctioned fights
- obey stop or retreat calls immediately

### Non-fighters

The most valuable thing non-fighters can do is simple:

> **Do not feed points.**

That means:

- shield up
- recall unnecessary fleets
- avoid dangerous zones during the window
- do not sit exposed during active combat
- do not provoke enemies in world chat

### Lower-power fighters

If they join, they still need discipline:

- stay with the group
- avoid direct solo collisions into stronger enemies
- do not drift away from the main objective

---

## 4. Recommended MIT2 pre-battle flow

### 24 hours before

Officers should confirm:

- who the opponent is
- what the active time windows are
- main and substitute roster
- commander assignment
- diplomacy rules
- instructions for non-fighters

### Battle notice

The notice should include at least:

- start time
- unified time zone
- fighter roster
- main targets
- non-fighter instructions
- commander and assembly method

---

## 5. The execution discipline that matters most

Keep commands short and repeatable:

- group up
- primary target
- stop
- retreat
- defend
- do not chase

Every member should understand one thing:

> **This day is about guild score, not personal emotion.**

---

## 6. Recommended internal rule

> Duel Battlefield on VS Day 6 is a high-intensity PvP event. Fighters must follow command. Non-fighters must shield and avoid feeding points. No member should provoke, solo attack, or hit diplomacy-protected targets without officer approval.

---

---

## 8. Final takeaway

The execution logic is straightforward:

1. **standardize the time reference**
2. **lock the fighter roster**
3. **protect non-fighters first**
4. **follow command over impulse**
