> Audience: members and officers preparing for SvS / VS Guild Duel  
> Purpose: turn the latest in-game screenshots into searchable text and a usable prep checklist  
> Updated: 2026-05-17

## Bottom line first

This SvS cycle is very straightforward:

- **Day 1**: construction / shipbuilding / rallies / PvE kills
- **Day 2**: champions / weapon fragments / commissions
- **Day 3**: research / crew tech / deep space items / ruins
- **Day 4**: flagship / blueprints / prismatic cores / commissions
- **Day 5**: combined release day for Days 1-4 resources
- **Day 6**: cross-server PvP / fortress presence / loss-for-points combat

So the core rule is still the same:

**do not spend key resources randomly during the week; release them on the matching scoring day.**

## What public community / official material seems to agree on

After checking currently searchable public sources, the pattern is consistent:

1. **High-value event progress in this game is strongly guild-coordinated.** The official beginner guide explicitly pushes players toward active guild participation instead of solo play.  
2. **Publicly indexed SvS day-by-day scoring tables are still sparse.** Your screenshots are more complete than most public references I could find.  
3. **The common prep pattern is still resource hoarding for the matching day.** That especially applies to champion fragments, flagship materials, research items, deep-space items, and ruin actions.  
4. **Day 6 is fundamentally a controlled risk-for-points combat day.** Existing guides and public discussion alike emphasize command discipline for fighters and shielding discipline for non-fighters.

## Practical prep checklist

### Before Day 1
- save construction speedups and shipbuilding speedups
- hold some trade shipping turns
- keep rally stamina / PvE time for Ascendancy Minions and Tribute Vessels

### Before Day 2
- do not spend champion fragments early
- hold weapon energy cores and epic / legendary weapon fragments
- try to time better commissions for the day

### Before Day 3
- hold research speedups
- do not casually burn Computational Components, Deep Space Beacons, Echo Modules, or Echoes of Deep Space
- try to save ruin excavation / plunder actions for the day

### Before Day 4
- hold flagship blueprints and prismatic cores
- delay major flagship upgrade / advancement actions if possible
- keep using commissions as filler score

### Before Day 5
- this is the broad release day for leftover value from earlier categories
- officers should ideally pre-announce who is pushing score and who is preserving stock

### Before Day 6
- fighters: prepare main fleets, repairs, teleports, and speedups
- non-fighters: shield up, recall fleets, stay out of risky zones
- avoid uncontrolled losses; if there is a caller, follow calls
