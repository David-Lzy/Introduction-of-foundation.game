# Shadowfront Guild Operations

> Audience: MIT2 / HuaXia officers and fighters  
> Role: guild-scale PvP control-point event  
> Updated: 2026-05-02

## One-line summary

**Shadowfront is not a spontaneous brawl. It is a scheduled guild battlefield where sign-up, roster discipline, vault control, and timing matter more than random fighting.**

---

## 1. Event role

Shadowfront is a multi-guild battlefield. The objective is not simply to score kills, but to:

- capture Vaults
- hold ownership through key scoring windows
- keep the main group coordinated
- convert battlefield control into guild ranking points

For MIT2, the biggest failure points are usually not power gaps, but:

- loose sign-up discipline
- unclear main/substitute assignments
- players scattering after entry
- no one holding a key Vault during the scoring phase

---

## 2. What we can safely confirm

Based on the current screenshots and compiled notes, this is the working model:

- up to **4 Commerce Guilds** can enter the same match
- each guild normally fields **20 main members**, with substitutes arranged separately
- there may be **Squad 1 / Squad 2** style scheduling
- scoring revolves around **Vault ownership** and major timing phases such as **Brief Awakening / Phase Eruption**
- defeated fleets usually respawn at an outer outpost rather than being removed permanently

---

## 4. Why Vault control matters more than random fights

### 4.1 Vaults

Vaults are the battlefield core objective. The usual logic is:

1. move Flagships onto the Vault area
2. maintain a living ship-count advantage around it
3. secure ownership and keep scoring
4. defend it until the next swing or scoring phase

The practical conclusion is simple:

> **Taking a Vault is only step one. Holding it through the right timing window is what actually wins points.**

### 4.2 Brief Awakening / scoring phases

The most important moment is often not the opening clash, but the later scoring phase tied to Vault timing.
For field discipline, members should understand:

- return to the target Vault before the next major scoring window
- do not chase isolated targets too far
- group cohesion is worth more than one extra personal kill

---

## 5. Expectations for participants

### Everyone should

- vote or sign up before officers finalize the roster
- be online 5-10 minutes early
- follow one commander once inside
- return to the group quickly after respawn
- stay focused on assigned Vaults

### What not to do

- demand a slot without signing up
- AFK after entering
- split off for solo duels
- tilt after one defeat and leave formation
- abandon the objective for personal contribution farming
- leave the battlefield completely unless command agrees

### Lower-power members still help

They are useful when they:

- stay with the main group
- reinforce numbers on key objectives
- fill timing gaps
- avoid meaningless solo fights

---

## 6. Recommended MIT2 organization flow

### Step 1: run sign-up first

Use a simple sign-up poll before officers lock the roster.

```text
Shadowfront Sign-up

Choose the time slot you can attend:
1. Squad 1
2. Squad 2
3. Either slot works
4. Unsure, but available as substitute
5. Cannot attend this round
```

### Step 2: build the roster

At minimum, officers should assign:

- main roster
- substitute roster
- one overall commander
- one Chinese coordinator
- one English coordinator

### Step 3: send a pre-battle notice

Send it 12-24 hours in advance with:

- start time
- unified time zone reference
- main and substitute roster
- rally / assembly time
- tactical focus
- basic do-not-do rules

### Step 4: keep execution simple

Use short commands only:

- group up
- move to target
- hold point
- stop chase
- return to Vault
- regroup after death

### Step 5: review after the match

Track at least:

- final rank
- opponent guilds
- attendance rate
- substitute usage
- main mistakes
- whether the next round needs a different slot or commander

---

## 7. Three lines every member should remember

> 1. Stay with the main group.  
> 2. Hit the target the commander calls.  
> 3. Be back on the key Vault before the next scoring phase.

---

## 8. Recommended internal rule

> Shadowfront is a guild objective event, not free-form PvP. Members who do not sign up, cannot confirm attendance, or do not reliably follow command should not be prioritized for main slots.
