# Calamity Boss Event

The point of this event is not "see the boss, rush the boss."

It is:

> **watch the spawn timer, track your cumulative damage target, and stop confusing daily loot cap with total-cycle value.**

If you manage the tempo correctly, Calamity becomes a steady source of **Cocoon** shards, generic Legendary shards, and Flagship material value.

---

## 1. What the event actually is

During the event window, a wormhole appears in the star system.
After the countdown ends, a **Calamity-grade** target spawns.

Structurally, this is closer to a shared world-boss event:

- multiple players can attack it
- rewards care more about **cumulative damage** than last-hit timing
- value comes from three layers: **damage rewards**, **cycle kill rewards**, and **daily normal loot**

So this is not a one-burst event.
The real loop is:

1. locate the wormhole
2. arrive before spawn
3. keep full fleets dealing steady damage
4. collect both cycle kills and the day's normal loot cap

---

## 2. Fastest way to enter cleanly

### 1) Use the event page to locate it

The source page explicitly says you can use **Locate** from the event panel to jump to the wormhole area.

So do not waste time free-scanning the whole map unless you want extra context.

### 2) Use the countdown as setup time

The wormhole appears before the fight starts, and the panel shows a spawn countdown.

That prep window should be used for:

- switching your main Flagship to the right combat school
- checking hero levels / stars / skills
- repairing and refilling fighters

Do not wait until the boss is already up to fix ships.

---

## 3. Key combat rule: this is not a one-ship snipe event

### 1) Put as many real fleets on target as possible

The most important mechanical detail from the source page is:

- **all fleets can attack the Calamity-grade target at the same time**

That means efficiency is not about gaming the last hit.
It is about:

> **keeping more damage-capable fleets active at once.**

### 2) Keep your combat school aligned

If your Flagship, heroes, and fighters are split across mismatched schools, paper power can look fine while real damage underperforms.

Best default:

- one clear combat school on your main Flagship
- heroes matched to that school
- fighters not randomly mixed

### 3) Repair before output collapses

The source page specifically warns that repeated fighting will wear down both fighters and the Flagship.

This matters because the event rewards **total damage dealt**.
Losing uptime is often worse than spending one more repair cycle.

---

## 4. Reward thresholds that actually matter

### 1) Cumulative damage rewards

The source page gives these key breakpoints:

- **400,000 to 30,000,000**: Cocoon shard ×1
- **40,000,000 to 100,000,000**: Cocoon shard ×2
- **120,000,000 to 150,000,000**: generic Legendary shard ×2
- **300,000,000 to 400,000,000**: generic Legendary shard ×3
- **500,000,000**: generic Legendary shard ×5

For practical planning, split it into three goal bands:

- **floor band**: at least touch **40,000,000**
- **practical value band**: push past **120,000,000**
- **heavy-investment band**: **300,000,000+**

For most non-whale players, **120,000,000** is the most realistic meaningful target.

### 2) Cycle kill rewards

Within one Calamity event cycle, extra loot is granted when the event accumulates:

- **3 kills**
- **5 kills**

So do not think only in single-run drops.
There is real value in staying present across the full cycle.

### 3) Daily normal loot cap

The source page is clear here too:

- **normal loot is capped at 5 per day**

Once you hit that cap, extra runs are mostly for cumulative damage progress or helping guildmates, not for more normal daily loot.

---

## 5. Best simple execution order

If you just want the clean routine:

1. use the event page to locate the wormhole
2. during countdown, repair, restack, and refill fighters
3. once the boss spawns, attach all real combat fleets first
4. check whether you are already close to the **5-loot daily cap**
5. decide whether today's goal is **40M**, **120M**, or **300M+** cumulative damage
6. if your Commerce Guild is active, stay through full kill cycles to collect the **3-kill / 5-kill** value

---

## One-line takeaway

**Calamity rewards come from early positioning, multi-fleet uptime, and understanding the difference between the 5-loot daily cap and the cumulative damage ladder.**
