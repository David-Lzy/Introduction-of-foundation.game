# Arms Race

> Audience: MIT2 / HuaXia officers and members  
> Role: manually started or scheduled cooperative guild PvE event  
> Updated: 2026-05-03

## One-line summary

**Arms Race should not be launched just because cooldown is over. Morale, main-roster attendance, and the A/B sequence matter first.**

---

## 1. Event role

Arms Race is a guild-organized challenge against powerful mock enemies. It depends on:

- officer timing
- coordinated damage output
- difficulty selection
- pre-event preparation

From the current event panel material, we can already confirm:

- officers can **start it manually or schedule it**
- there are **Arms Race A / Arms Race B** branches or stages
- **Morale** is part of the preparation system
- difficulty choice directly affects stability and reward efficiency

---

## 2. The three checks that matter most

### 2.1 Morale

The screenshots show Morale level, Morale progress, and a way to improve it. That means Morale is not cosmetic - it is part of the launch decision.

### 2.2 Main attendance

If the main damage dealers are offline, fleets are tied up elsewhere, or several players are already busy with another event, a rushed launch can:

- slow the clear
- reduce success consistency
- waste cooldown
- disrupt the guild's overall rhythm

### 2.3 A/B sequence clarity

Current information suggests Arms Race B may depend on opening or clearing A first. Officers should align on:

- whether the plan is A only or A into B
- whether conditions are strong enough for both
- whether the notice should explain the expected sequence up front

---

## 3. How to phrase "twice per week"

Some third-party material suggests Arms Race may have roughly a 3-day cooldown, which makes **two runs in one week possible in theory**.

The safer guild wording is:

> **Arms Race may be scheduled twice in a week when cooldown and attendance allow, but the real frequency should follow the live server cooldown, event conflicts, and roster availability.**

Do not present it as a fixed weekly rule.

---

## 4. Expectations for members

### Everyone should

- help raise Morale when preparation is open
- be online around 10 minutes early
- keep a main fleet available
- watch guild chat and officer markers
- avoid self-starting or pressure-spamming officers

### High-power members should

- lead Morale preparation
- provide main event damage
- focus dangerous or high-difficulty targets
- help test the highest stable difficulty the guild can handle
- keep reliable rallies open so others can drop out, repair, and rejoin

### Lower-power members should

- join the preparation phase when possible
- stay with the main group
- avoid opening the wrong rally or pulling targets badly
- follow instructions on whether to join higher-difficulty stages
- prioritize joining rallies opened by stronger players instead of improvising alone

### Rally sustain trick (newly added)

This is a practical Arms Race tactic confirmed in guild chat and worth documenting clearly:

1. **Let stronger players open the rally, and have lower-power players join that rally.**
2. **Do not wait until your ship is nearly destroyed. When HP drops to around half, leave the rally.**
3. **Repair immediately, then rejoin the rally.**
4. **This helps maximize total damage over the full run and reduces how often the rally leader has to reform.**
5. **This is not only for sub-20 players. It is useful for everyone, though lower-power players need the reminder most.**

### Why this works

- it keeps damage output stable for longer
- it reduces dead time caused by ships exploding one by one
- it lowers the burden on the rally leader to keep rebuilding rallies
- it is especially helpful for lower-power members who would otherwise lose ships too early

### Execution reminders

- officers can explain this rule once before the event starts
- stronger players should keep reopening or maintaining rallies so members always have one to rejoin
- lower-power players should prepare to leave, repair, and return once HP is around half instead of tanking until destruction
- if the field gets messy, follow live calls from the guild leader or R4 officers first

### Multi-rally population balancing (added follow-up)

If your guild usually runs **around five rallies at the same time**, members should not just join whichever rally appears first on screen. The better rule is to distribute people across the active rallies.

1. **Wait until most of the planned rally list is visible before choosing.**
2. **Prefer the rallies with fewer members instead of overloading the ones already close to full.**
3. **The goal is not to fill one rally as fast as possible, but to keep total headcount across rallies reasonably even.**
4. **If one rally only has 1-2 members while others are nearly full, members should reinforce the underfilled rally first.**
5. **If auto-join sends people into the wrong rally, adjust manually when needed so you do not end up with one packed rally and several weak ones.**

### Why this matters

- it keeps damage output more even across all five rallies
- it reduces the risk of some rallies underperforming simply because they are understaffed
- it prevents fast clickers from unintentionally stacking only the top one or two rallies
- it gives officers a clearer view of which rally still needs support

## 5. Recommended MIT2 launch flow

### Pre-launch checklist

- [ ] cooldown finished
- [ ] no major clash with VS / Shadowfront / Port events
- [ ] Morale reached the target
- [ ] main roster online
- [ ] A/B sequence confirmed
- [ ] target difficulty confirmed
- [ ] announcement already sent

### Recommended flow

1. confirm availability
2. organize Morale preparation first
3. set difficulty based on attendance
4. officer schedules or opens Arms Race A
5. unify markers and focus fire during the run
6. decide after A whether B should follow
7. log participation and result afterward

---

## 6. Recommended internal rule

> Arms Race should be scheduled or opened only by the guild leader or designated R4 officers. Before launch, officers should confirm cooldown, Morale, main attendance, and event conflicts. Members are expected to help with Morale preparation and follow officer markers during the run.
