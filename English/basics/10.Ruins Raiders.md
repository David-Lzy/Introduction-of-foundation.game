# Ruins Raiders

The point of this system is not "tap it when you remember it." 
The real value comes from understanding **excavation**, **daily raid limits**, and the **integrity gate**.

---

## 1. What this system actually is

**Ruins Raiders** is a two-part system:

- you send fleets to **excavate** ruins
- you wait and collect rewards later
- you can also **raid** other players' ruins

The source page adds two very useful basics:

1. it unlocks at **Energy Core Lv.13**
2. excavation uses fleets, but it **does not block your other commands**

So the practical takeaway is:

> once the feature is unlocked, you can keep all 3 fleets working there without treating it like a full activity lock.

---

## 2. How excavation works

### Entry
- open the space interface
- tap the **Ruins Raiders** icon on the left
- go to the **Excavate** tab

### Basic flow
1. choose a ruin node
2. check reward and duration
3. assign a **Flagship**
4. come back after the timer ends and claim

### Reward quality
The page says reward quality scales with ruin rarity:

- white
- green
- blue
- purple
- gold

Higher rarity means better rewards.

### Rerolling excavation targets
If you have **Ruin Maps**, you can refresh the ruin planet selection.

So this is not just "take whatever appears."
With maps, you can screen for better targets.

---

## 3. How raiding works

### Daily raid limit
The source gives a hard cap:

- **4 raids per day**

That means raid attempts are limited and should be spent on worthwhile targets.

### Basic flow
1. open the **Raid** tab
2. choose a target ruin
3. inspect the reward
4. win the fight
5. take part of that ruin's output

### Rerolling targets
If the current raid list is weak, you can refresh it.

---

## 4. The key gate: below 50% integrity, the ruin cannot be raided

The page states:

- raided ruins lose **integrity**
- at **50% integrity or lower**, the ruin cannot be raided again

That matters a lot in practice:

> if the target is already too damaged, do not waste one of your 4 daily raids on it.

---

## 5. How rewards are calculated

### Excavation rewards
Typical rewards include:

- basic resources
- speedups
- Credits
- occasional Ruin Maps

But there is a risk:

- if someone raids your ruin
- your final claim gets reduced

### Raid rewards
The source gives specific numbers:

- a successful raid takes **25% of the resource rewards**
- plus **1-2 random items** from that ruin's excavation reward pool

That is why the system matters.
It is both a passive income feature and a targetable economy feature.

---

## 6. Best everyday use

### For growth-focused accounts
Recommended order:

1. keep excavation slots running consistently
2. use **Ruin Maps** to hunt better rarity when you have them
3. spend raid attempts only on good-value targets

### During score/event days
If your event schedule values ruin actions, try to line up:

- excavation claims
- raid attempts

with the correct scoring day.

---

## 7. Common mistakes

### Mistake 1: only assigning 1 fleet
No need.
The source explicitly says excavation can run alongside other commands.

### Mistake 2: spending raid attempts casually
Wrong.
You only get **4** per day.

### Mistake 3: ignoring integrity
Wrong.
At **50% or lower**, that target cannot be raided.

---

## One-line takeaway

> **Ruins Raiders is a two-track system: keep excavation running for baseline value, then spend your 4 daily raids on high-value targets that are still above the 50% integrity floor.**
