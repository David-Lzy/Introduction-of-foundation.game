# Daily and Weekly Routine Checklist

This page does one job:
turn scattered reset timers, claim caps, and side systems into a routine you can actually follow.

If your playtime is irregular, prioritize the items that are both **high value** and **easy to overflow**.

---

## 1) Highest-priority daily actions

Start with the things that directly lose value if ignored:

- **free summons** so cooldown does not sit idle
- **daily missions**, with **120 points** as the default target
- mothership **Water / Metal / food** collection
- **Rian** for **50 Action Points (AP)** every day
- **Deep Space Echo** collection before the **12-hour** cap overflows
- **Repair Dock quick production**: **1 free use per day**

These are the foundation of a good routine because:
- skipping them is a direct loss
- the return is stable
- they require very little judgment

---

## 2) System-by-system daily routine

### Daily missions: treat 120 as the baseline

The source page lists common mission buckets such as:

- defeating Tribute Ships
- defeating Supreme Prayer Ships
- Merchant Network tasks
- Trade Route orders
- Deep Space Echo challenges
- Holo Arena
- building upgrades
- hero recruitment

The practical approach is:

1. do the systems you already needed to do
2. then backfill the missing mission points
3. try to reach **120** before logging off

> This is more resource-efficient than tunnel-visioning one mission at a time, because many tasks complete naturally on the way.

### Mothership resources: do not collect only once

Your resource facilities keep producing:

- **Water Circulation System** → Water
- **Refinery** → Metal
- **Food Processing Room** → food

If you log in only once a day, collect immediately.
If you log in multiple times, split the claims so storage does not sit capped for long periods.

### Rian: fixed 50 AP every day

Location: in front of the **Energy Core**.

This is one of the easiest stable gains to forget.
If you spend AP on Tribute Ships, Supreme Vanguard targets, or Merchant Network PvE requests, the extra **50 AP** matters.

### Deep Space Echo: schedule around the 12-hour cap

The source page is explicit:

- rewards accumulate hourly
- storage caps at **12 hours**

So this should not be a random “claim when remembered” system.
A simple habit is:

- claim once in the morning
- claim once at night

### Repair Dock: use the free quick production every day

The Repair Dock gives **1 quick production** each day.
Its output feeds **Universal Repair Modules**, which are consumed over the long term by Flagship and fighter repairs.

This resource may not feel tight immediately,
but it often becomes a sudden bottleneck during PvP or event-heavy periods.
That is why it belongs in the normal routine.

### Holo Arena: spend the 3 daily tickets

The source page states:

- arena tickets recover to **3 per day**
- reset timing is listed as **09:00 JST**

Use the in-game timer as the final authority,
but the working habit is simple:

> **Do not let arena tickets overflow. Fight at least 3 times per day.**

### Commerce Guild shop free item: claim it daily

The source page lists:

- refresh at **09:00 JST**
- **1 free daily item**

The value of one claim is not always dramatic,
but the click cost is tiny, so it should be folded into the standard routine.

### Merchant Network: watch the four refresh windows

The source page lists these refresh times in Japan time:

- **03:00**
- **09:00**
- **15:00**
- **21:00**

It also gives one important expiry rule:

- requests disappear after **18 hours** if unfinished

So the right mindset is not “check when the red dot appears,” but:

1. check whether high-quality requests are up
2. clear the ones close to expiring first
3. then decide how much AP to leave for Tribute Ships or Supreme Vanguard targets

### Trade Routes: keep ships working every day

Trade matters because it:

- keeps producing **Galactic Coin** and resources
- converts offline time into progress

The source page adds two easy-to-miss details:

- **2 investments per day**
- **2 hacks per day**

If you are not playing aggressive PvP, the minimum good habit is:

- do not leave your main transport slot idle
- start a long run before logging off

### Supreme Prayer Ship / Supreme Vanguard / Tribute Ship

The page gives these hard numbers:

- **Supreme Prayer Ship**: rewards can be claimed **up to 3 times per day**
- **Supreme Vanguard**: costs **10 AP**
- **Tribute Ship**: costs **30 AP**

Recommended priority:

1. secure your fixed daily claims first
2. then use remaining AP on these PvE targets
3. do not leave Tribute Ships until the very end of your session unless that is intentional

## 3) Compressed routine when time is short

If you only have 10–15 minutes, do this:

1. collect mothership resources
2. claim **50 AP** from **Rian**
3. use free summons
4. trigger Repair Dock quick production
5. claim the Commerce Guild shop free item
6. clear one round of high-value Merchant Network / daily mission progress
7. start Trade Routes
8. spend any Holo Arena tickets or Deep Space Echo claims close to overflow

The goal is not to finish everything.
It is to protect the value that disappears most easily.

---

## 4) Weekly checklist

### Weekly mission target: 330

The weekly structure on the source page includes:

- login days
- Commerce Guild donation
- speedup usage
- fighter production
- Credits spending
- hero recruitment
- Merchant Network clears
- Tribute Ships
- Supreme Prayer Ships
- Trade Route orders
- Deep Space Echo
- Holo Arena
- relic excavation

The practical default target is:

> **Reach 330 every week.**

That usually means your core progression systems are not being neglected for too long.

### Weekly free items: remember Monday reset

The page also notes:

- weekly free items in the Commerce Guild shop and some other stores
- refresh at **09:00 JST on Monday**

If you tend to forget, the simplest rule is:

- treat **Monday shop check** as a fixed routine action

---

## 5) Bottom line

An efficient routine is not about clicking every button.
It is about protecting three kinds of value first:

- rewards that overflow
- rewards with daily claim caps
- fixed resources that quietly bottleneck progression later

Lock those down first, and you stop losing value in the background even on busy days.
